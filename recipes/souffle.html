Swedish Chef's Lighter Than Air Soufflé

About the Recipe

← Back to Recipes

"Yehmbørndêr døyür bedîskedoør, yî bøør gëdürdeyürtmm! Børk, børk, børk!"
A gravity-defying masterpiece that may require archery skills.

Preparation Time:
30 mins
Cooking Time:
18 mins
Yield:
Serves 4

Recipe Content

Ingredients:

  • 1 basket of potatoes and carrots (for throwing, not for cooking)
  • 3 cups soufflé batter (whatever that is, it's fluffy)
  • 1 bow and arrow (for retrieval purposes)
  • A deep sense of resignation
Actual ingredients

Ingredients:

Base:

  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (57g) sugar, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (115ml) whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter (plus extra for greasing)
  • 1 cup (170g) bittersweet chocolate, chopped
  • 4 egg yolks (room temperature)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Whipped Egg Whites:

  • 4 egg whites (room temperature)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons sugar (for egg whites)
  • Powdered sugar (for dusting, optional)

Instructions:

  1. "Yehmbørndêr døyür bedîskedoør, yî bøør gëdürdeyürtmm! Børk, børk, børk!" – Shake a basket of potatoes and carrots enthusiastically. Fling them behind you to test their weightlessness.
  2. "Dürnbësht hûûrdê dê hêd hîmmî hûmmî soofleeyh! Mmm dê pîhpî pööfî sooflîn! Î, yîïï!" – Mix your soufflé batter with fluffy, poofy precision. Carefully pour it into your dish and place it in the oven. Watch carefully, as things may soon get out of control.
  3. "Oøøh, soofleeyh! Soofleeyh, koom-koom! Koombegeür! Hêêrê hëë! Shûûrleekøh, koom dööwn! Uuuuuh...." – Your soufflé may attempt to levitate. Try calling it back. If it begins floating out of the dish, do not attempt to grab it with your hands—it is already beyond your control.
  4. "Éêhrn gøhnee-whyyës yîtdeh yûûrnms gøørgëëzhh koom—shûûrleekøh!" – JIf the soufflé refuses to return, it's time for the bow and arrow. Aim carefully! You must skewer the soufflé mid-air and bring it back to its dish. If successful, you may continue with plating.
  5. "Uh freezbee shøøfleeyh!" – Congratulations! You have now invented the Frisbee Soufflé.
Actual steps

Instructions:

Prepare the Ramekins:

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease 4 ramekins with butter and coat with sugar, tapping out the excess.

Make the Base:

  1. In a medium saucepan, whisk together flour, salt, and 2 tablespoons of sugar. Gradually whisk in milk until smooth.
  2. Over medium heat, add butter and whisk until the mixture thickens (about 2–3 minutes).
  3. Remove from heat and stir in chocolate until smooth. Let the mixture cool slightly.
  4. Stir in egg yolks and vanilla extract. Set aside to cool.

Whip the Egg Whites:

  1. In a clean bowl, beat egg whites and cream of tartar (if using) on low speed until foamy.
  2. Increase the speed to high and gradually add 2 tablespoons of sugar, one tablespoon at a time, until stiff, glossy peaks form.

Fold & Combine:

  1. Fold one-third of the egg whites into the chocolate mixture to lighten it.
  2. Carefully fold in the remaining egg whites, one-third at a time, until no streaks remain and the mixture is smooth.

Bake the Soufflé:

  1. Divide the batter evenly among the ramekins, filling them to the top. Smooth the tops with a spatula.
  2. Bake for 16-18 minutes until the soufflés are puffed and the tops are lightly browned. The center should not wobble too much.

Serve Immediately:

  1. Dust with powdered sugar and serve warm. Enjoy!
